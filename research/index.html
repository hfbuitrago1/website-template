Perceptions of Inequality predict Americans' Level of Support for Redistribution

Although the levels of economic inequality in the United States have increased dramatically, Americans' support for redistributive policies remains low. In four studies, we examine whether support for redistributive policies is related to perceptions of economic inequality. We hypothesized that Americans' tendency to underestimate economic inequality will decrease support for redistributive policies by increasing their belief in upward economic mobility. In Study 1, we found that participants consistently underestimate the scope of economic inequality in the United States. Moreover, participants who perceived the distribution of wealth as less unequal showed lower support for policies aimed to reduce economic inequality (studies 2 and 3). In Study 4, we examine whether Americans' belief in upward economic mobility mediated this relationship. As estimates of economic inequality decreased, we found that lower support for redistributive policies was mediated by participants' belief in upward economic mobility. Together, our findings underscore the importance of acknowledging the actual levels of economic inequality to increase Americansâ€™ support for redistributive justice.
