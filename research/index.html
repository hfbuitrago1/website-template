  1. Perceptions of Inequality and Support for Redistributive Policies Buitrago, Felipe
    Although the wealth gap in the United States has increased dramatically, Americans’ support for redistributive policies remains low. This research examines whether the level of support for redistributive policies is related to perceptions of economic inequality. Across four studies, we found that participants consistently underestimate the wealth gap in the United States. Furthermore, participants who perceive the distribution of wealth as less unequal show lower support for policies aimed at reducing economic inequality. Consequently, making the wealth gap in the United States salient increases participants’ support for redistributive policies. In upcoming studies, we will examine the causal mechanisms that underlie these relationships.
